# Milestone 3.6 — Volatility Forecasting

**Project:** Stock Market Trend Analysis | **Notebook:** `notebooks/04_volatility_modeling.py` | **Seed:** 42
**Reads:** `train_fe` + `val_fe` for model selection; `holdout_fe` opened **exactly once** at §7.
**Not investment advice.** A volatility forecast is a *risk* estimate (option pricing, position sizing, VaR),
**not** a directional trading signal — predictable variance does not imply predictable return.

## Headline (honest)

Where next-day **return direction** was an efficient-market coin flip (M3/M4), **realized volatility is
genuinely predictable out-of-sample.** On the sealed 2025→2026 holdout, every real model beats a
random-walk-volatility floor on QLIKE with tiny p-values at **both** the 5-day and 20-day horizons, and the
best models also beat the standard HAR-RV benchmark. This is the project's first economically meaningful
positive result — and it does **not** contradict the EMH conclusion, because it is about the second moment
(risk), not the first (return).

## Why volatility (the pivot)

M2 already documented the mechanism: **volatility clustering** (ACF of squared returns lag-1 +0.487),
a **leverage effect**, and conditional heteroskedasticity (ARCH-LM significant on all tickers). M3's
GJR-GARCH standardised residuals came out white (Ljung-Box p > 0.29) — the variance dynamics were
captured. A return-horizon probe (`scripts/horizon_experiment.py`) confirmed 5-/20-day return *direction*
adds no usable edge (the apparent signal was upward drift + overlapping-window pseudo-replication). So the
predictable structure lives in **variance**, and this milestone forecasts it directly.

## Target (leakage-safe, built in M1)

`fwd_rv_h` = sqrt( Σ log_return² over days t+1 … t+h ), per ticker, per split, via `shift(-h)`. The last h
rows of each split are NaN (their forward window would cross the split edge), asserted by an exactly-h
tail-NaN guard in notebook 01. Modelled in **log space** (RV is right-skewed) and back-transformed with the
**Duan smearing** correction. Horizons: **h = 5 (primary), h = 20 (secondary).**

## Models

| Model | What it is |
|---|---|
| **RandomWalk-vol** | Predict next h-day RV with the current trailing h-day RV (persistence floor). |
| **HAR-RV (OLS)** | Regress `log(fwd_rv_h)` on `log(realized_vol_5/21/63)` — the standard volatility benchmark. |
| **GJR-GARCH(1,1)-t** | Per ticker (M3 spec, `o=1` asymmetry); h-day variance = sum of the h daily variance forecasts. |
| **LightGBM (log-RV)** | On `MODEL_FEATURES` + ticker; Optuna over a **horizon-aware** purged walk-forward CV (embargo = h). |

## Honest reading

1. **The positive result is real and robust.** Every real model beats the random-walk floor on QLIKE with
   p ≤ 1e-9 (h=5) and p ≤ 0.033 (h=20) out-of-sample. Volatility *is* forecastable here.
2. **Best out-of-sample model.** At h=5, GJR-GARCH and LightGBM are a **statistical near-tie** (QLIKE
   −5.467 vs −5.462); LightGBM wins on R² (0.41 vs 0.33). At h=20, LightGBM is clearly best and — unlike on
   val — **beats HAR significantly (p=0.005)**, so the val-set HAR tie did not persist out-of-sample.
   LightGBM is the most consistent across horizons; GARCH is its equal at h=5.
3. **R² drops from val to holdout** (h=5: 0.57→0.41, h=20: 0.70→0.59). The holdout RV *distribution* is
   actually near-identical to train (mean 0.038 vs 0.039, p95 0.085 vs 0.094), so this is mild distribution
   shift / finite-sample variation, **not** a volatility-regime change. The skill stays strongly positive and
   significant OOS — no overfitting alarm.
4. **Mincer–Zarnowitz slopes are below 1** (0.71–0.92 OOS): forecasts are mildly **over-dispersed** (a touch
   too reactive). A simple shrinkage/recalibration could tighten this — noted, not fixed (out of scope).
5. **What this is NOT.** Predictable variance ≠ tradeable return alpha. The value is in risk management
   (position sizing, option pricing, VaR/stress) — consistent with, not contradicting, the M3/M4 EMH finding
   on returns.

## Methods & leakage discipline

- Target built per-split; forward window provably `[t+1, t+h]` (day t excluded), verified against raw
  returns to machine epsilon. Holdout read on exactly one guarded line.
- LightGBM CV: purged expanding walk-forward, **embargo tied to horizon** (a 1-day embargo on a multi-day
  target is structurally impossible; a load-bearing purge-count assert fires otherwise).
- GARCH fit excludes the eval split (`last_obs` = first eval date; verified train-only refit gives identical
  params). All `MODEL_FEATURES` are past-only (`shift(1).rolling()`); same-day return/VIX are legitimately
  known at t.
- Metrics: QLIKE decisive; block-aware DM (Newey–West lag = h-1) so overlapping h-day windows don't inflate
  significance; Duan smearing on every log→level back-transform; one common dropna mask per horizon.

## Corrections (from two adversarial Model-QA audits)

- **Phase 1 (targets):** tightened the leakage guard to assert exactly h tail-NaN rows/ticker (catches a
  sub-h-ticker vacuity and interior-NaN voids); corrected a plan mis-statement into a hard leak-trap warning.
- **Phase 2 (models):** made the embargo assert load-bearing (tie embargo to horizon); surfaced DM-vs-HAR in
  the summary so the h=20 val tie could not be buried; documented that R²_log is on the smeared level
  (conservative, never inflated). Audit verdict: **leakage-safe; 5-day skill honest and reproducible**
  (DM survives 4× HAC widening).
