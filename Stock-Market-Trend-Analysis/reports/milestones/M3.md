## M3.5 hardening pass — feature ablation + Optuna tuning + ensemble fix (2026-07-05)

A Senior-ML-Engineer review pass, itself **adversarially audited** by three subagents (Model QA, Investment
Researcher, Data Engineer) whose findings were applied before these numbers were finalised. Goal: **maximise
out-of-sample performance while staying academically rigorous** — not add models blindly. The project's own M2
evidence says the binding constraint is *signal*, not model capacity (next-day **variance** is predictable,
next-day **direction** is near-noise, Pearson |r| < 0.075), so the honest move is to fix the real methodological
gaps and test every addition on the sealed holdout, keeping only what beats it. **Scope (user-chosen): rigorous
ablation · no new model families · variance-axis features only.**

### 1. Fixed the RSI + added a small variance-axis candidate pool

`rsi_14` was a simple rolling-mean gain/loss but mislabeled "Wilder RSI" and, uniquely among the rolling
features, was **not** `.shift(1)`-ed. Fixed to a true Wilder EMA (`ewm(alpha=1/14)`) with `.shift(1)` — a
correctness fix (RSI's OOS value is ~0 either way, direction is noise). (The EMA is seeded on the first delta,
not an SMA of the first *n* gains as canonical Wilder does; the two differ only over the dropped warm-up.) Added
five **candidate** variance-axis features (persisted but gated): `semi_vol_21/63`, `ret_skew_21`, `ret_kurt_21`,
`atr_14` (normalized ATR). MACD / Bollinger / EMA / OBV were **not** added — they are collinear transforms of the
direction axis the model already exhausted, i.e. pure overfitting surface. (`atr_14` was also fixed during the
audit: it originally differenced RAW High/Low against the ADJUSTED close, inflating pre-split NVDA history ~10–40×;
now H/L are put on the adjusted basis first.)

### 2. Leakage-safe feature ablation (train-only) — ALL candidates rejected on evidence

A train-only harness ranks each candidate by **(a)** mutual information vs the next-day target above a
shuffled-target noise floor and **(b)** walk-forward permutation importance (does LightGBM's OOS RMSE *rise* when
the feature is shuffled, across 5 purged expanding folds). A candidate is kept only if it clears the MI floor
**and** its permutation benefit **exceeds its own cross-fold noise** (`perm_dRMSE > perm_std`) — a mean that is
merely positive-by-a-hair is indistinguishable from zero. Val and holdout are never read.

| candidate | MI | MI floor+3σ | perm ΔRMSE (±σ) | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `semi_vol_21` | 0.101 | 0.020 | −0.0000310 (±0.00004) | reject (redundant with `realized_vol`/`parkinson`) |
| `semi_vol_63` | 0.120 | 0.020 | −0.0000765 (±0.00018) | reject (redundant) |
| `ret_skew_21` | 0.011 | 0.020 | +0.0000005 (±0.00003) | reject (below MI floor + noise) |
| `ret_kurt_21` | 0.000 | 0.020 | +0.0000250 (±0.00001) | reject (below MI floor) |
| `atr_14` | 0.152 | 0.020 | −0.0000324 (±0.00011) | reject (permutation *helps* → redundant) |

Result: **0 of 5 kept.** `FEAT_MODEL` = the 39 baseline features, unchanged. Every candidate has real MI (they
*are* on the predictable variance axis), but the existing `realized_vol`/`parkinson_vol` features already span
that axis — permuting the candidates does not raise OOS error, so they add only overfitting surface. This is the
"smaller, scientifically justified feature set" done to its logical end: the evidence supports keeping **nothing**
new, and the rejection is on record with numbers. (Rejected candidates stay in the parquet as documented
diagnostic columns, not in `X`.)

### 3. Optuna hyperparameter tuning on a purged walk-forward CV — the biggest fix

The baseline LightGBM was deliberately un-tuned (fixed 400 trees, one val peek) and **overfit**. Replaced that
with an Optuna TPE search (60 trials) whose objective is the **mean OOS RMSE across 5 purged expanding
walk-forward folds inside train** (split by date, 1-day embargo so the next-day label can't bleed across a fold
edge). `n_estimators` is set by per-fold early stopping (median), never tuned blindly. The objective structurally
**never reads val or holdout**, so tuning cannot see the metric it is later judged on. (Disclosed optimism: each
fold early-stops on the same OOS slice it scores, so the CV RMSE is mildly optimistic about tree count — contained
to train, and the promote decision rests on the sealed holdout.)

The CV chose a **radically shrunk** model: **2 trees**, `max_depth=3`, `min_child_samples=90`,
`feature_fraction=0.61`. That is the leakage-free CV correctly reporting that the signal supports almost no
model complexity — the exact opposite of the overfit 400-tree baseline. Because the ablation kept 0 features,
baseline and tuned models use the **same 39 features**, so this is a **clean tuning-only comparison** (no
feature/tuning confound).

### 4. Promote/revert guardrail on the sealed holdout — PROMOTE

Pre-registered metric = pooled holdout RMSE (DirAcc as tie context, Diebold-Mariano for significance). Holdout
opened once. PROMOTE only if the tuned model beats the baseline **and** that improvement is DM-significant
(the DM test is load-bearing, not decorative):

| LightGBM (39 feat) | holdout RMSE | vs naive (DM p) | DirAcc | DirAcc p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| baseline (400 trees, un-tuned) | 0.02168 | 0.003 (**WORSE** than naive) | 0.483 | 0.226 |
| **tuned (2 trees, walk-forward CV)** | **0.02107** | 0.533 (**ties** naive) | **0.542** | **0.002** |

- **tuned vs baseline: DM stat −3.02, p = 0.0025 → the improvement is statistically real → `PROMOTE tuned`.**
- What the fix bought (honestly): it **eliminated the overfit**. The baseline *lost* to naive (DM p=0.003 worse);
  the tuned model now **ties** naive on RMSE (0.02107 ≈ the 0.02109 naive floor). It still does **not beat** naive
  — the EMH ceiling is intact — but it no longer breaks against it, which is the defensible win, and it is a
  genuine DM-significant improvement *over the baseline model*.
- **Read the 0.542 directional figure with the same caveats §8 applies to GARCH/LSTM, not as new skill.** A
  2-tree model that ties naive on RMSE predicts ≈0; its *sign* is riding the same lag-1 mean-reversion
  microstructure GARCH-mean rides — tellingly all three land at ~0.542–0.543. It is (a) erased by transaction
  costs (§8 backtest), (b) one of **six** model variants now tested → multiple-comparison risk, (c) microstructure,
  not alpha. It is not a tuning "achievement," it is the overfit being removed so LightGBM stops actively losing.

### 5. Ensemble fixed — return-scale, honest shrinkage framing

The old ensemble z-scored LightGBM + LSTM (off-scale, RMSE reported n/a) and blended the *overfit* LightGBM into
the real-edge LSTM. Replaced with an **equal-weight, return-scale average of GJR-GARCH-mean + attention-LSTM**.
Honest description: GARCH's mean forecast is a near-zero, lag-1-driven **near-naive anchor**, so this blend is the
LSTM *shrunk halfway toward zero* — often helpful for near-zero-mean returns, not a fusion of two independent
alphas. Whether averaging genuinely helps depends on how decorrelated the members' errors are, so §8 **measures**
that holdout error correlation (Colab run) rather than asserting it. Now RMSE / MAE / DM are well-defined and it
feeds the costed backtest. Membership note: by the same significance test there are **three** holdout survivors
(GARCH, LSTM, tuned-LGB, all ~0.542); tuned-LGB is excluded because its lag-1 signal is redundant with GARCH-mean
— a stated judgment call. The old LGB+LSTM z-blend is retained only as the documented *rejected* baseline.

### 6. LSTM tuning (Colab-gated)

Added a small Optuna search (15 trials, `units/dropout/learning_rate/batch_size/layers/bidirectional`) over one
purged inner time-split (scaler refit on inner-train only), plus a `bidirectional` option in `build_lstm`. Gated
behind `FULL_TRAIN` so local stays a 2-epoch smoke and the GPU run tunes + refits 20 epochs (see
`COLAB_TRAINING_GUIDE.md`). **Caveat:** the 20-epoch LSTM holdout numbers (0.542, p=0.005) and the holdout
ensemble come from the GPU/forced run; the local default is a smoke test and the self-audit checks the smoke
artifact, so those two rows are "Colab, not locally auto-reproduced."

**Net M3.5:** the pipeline is smaller and scientifically stronger — **0 features added** (5 tested, all rejected
with numbers), a leakage-free walk-forward CV that replaced the one-val-peek selection, and a LightGBM that went
from worse-than-naive to naive-tying — a real, DM-significant OOS gain **over the baseline** (holdout still not
beaten, EMH intact). The ensemble is now fully scorable. Note the self-audit's **22/22 checks are structural**
(files/columns exist); they do not re-verify the headline numbers — those come from the printed run. The EMH
headline is unchanged, now *earned* by a rigorous protocol rather than a single un-tuned model. Artifacts:
`models/` `feature_ablation.csv`, `lgbm_best_params.json`, `lgbm_optuna_study.pkl`, `lgb_tuning_guardrail.json`,
`lstm_best_params.json`; figures `M3_fig_ablation.png`, `M3_fig_lgb_optuna.png`.

## Enhancement pass — exogenous data + LightGBM + attention + ensemble (2026-06-17)

Four advanced patterns were added to push for predictive lift. **Result: they add real in-sample/val signal
but confirm the out-of-sample ceiling — the more powerful model (LightGBM + exogenous data) actually
*overfits* the holdout.** That is itself the most instructive finding.

**1. Exogenous features (9 new, the only break from price-history-only).** Pulled VIX (fear/sentiment +
regime proxy), 10y/3m Treasury yields, and the dollar index from Yahoo via the same snapshot pipeline; merged
by date; engineered leakage-safe features (`vix_level, vix_log_change, vix_z_60, is_high_vol_regime,
tnx_level, tnx_change, term_spread, term_spread_change, dxy_log_return`). All are market-priced daily values
known at close *t* (no CPI-style release-lag lookahead). MODEL_FEATURES: 30 → **39**.

**2. LightGBM (tabular gradient-boosted trees), fully local.** Trained on the 39 features + `ticker`
categorical.

| LightGBM | RMSE | DirAcc | DirAcc p | DM vs naive |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **val** | 0.0207 | **0.539** | **0.015 (sig)** | 0.149 (ns) |
| **holdout** | 0.02165 | 0.499 | 0.979 | **0.003 (sig WORSE)** |

**8 of LightGBM's top-15 feature importances are the new exogenous features** (`vix_level`, `dxy_log_return`,
`vix_z_60`, `term_spread`, `tnx_level`, …) — so the exogenous data is genuinely *informative in-sample*. But
on the holdout LightGBM is **significantly worse than naive** (DM p=0.003) and directionally a coin flip
(0.499). **The exogenous features + model complexity bought a val edge that did not generalize — textbook
overfitting, and a sharper restatement of the EMH ceiling than ARIMA alone.**

**3. Attention LSTM.** The Keras model was upgraded with a Bahdanau-style **additive attention** layer that
weights all 60 timesteps (focus on informative days, e.g. a vol spike) instead of using only the last step.
Verified by a 2-epoch CPU smoke-test (runs end-to-end); full training is the Colab job — the real attention
result is still **pending**.

**4. Ensemble.** A z-scored **directional blend** of LightGBM + LSTM predictions (`zblend`). Reported
DirAcc-only (a z-blend is off the return scale, so RMSE is n/a by design). With the smoke LSTM it is
illustrative plumbing (DirAcc 0.518); the real blend awaits the Colab LSTM.

**Honest verdict on the enhancements:** the exogenous data is real, leakage-free, and demonstrably used — but
it **did not produce out-of-sample lift**. ARIMA's holdout numbers are unchanged; LightGBM overfit. This
*strengthens* the project's EMH conclusion: even good alternative data + a strong nonlinear model cannot beat
naive on next-day direction out-of-sample. New artifacts: `models/lgbm_global{,_final}.txt`,
`lstm_attention_smoke.keras`, `M3_fig_lgb_importance.png`; `holdout_predictions.parquet` gains `y_pred_lgb`.
Self-audit: **19/19**.

## 4. GJR-GARCH(1,1) Student-t — variance model, evaluated as a variance model

AR(1) mean + GJR-GARCH(1,1) (`o=1` asymmetry) + Student-t errors; out-of-sample via `last_obs`.

| Ticker | ν (t dof) | γ (leverage) | mean vol | QLIKE | MZ R² | std-resid LB p | std-resid² LB p |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `^GSPC` | 5.8 | **0.233** | 0.82% | −8.68 | 0.053 | 0.499 | 0.775 |
| `AAPL` | 5.6 | 0.127 | 1.53% | −7.48 | 0.001 | 0.294 | 0.962 |
| `AMZN` | 5.1 | 0.100 | 1.80% | −7.06 | 0.002 | 0.976 | 0.994 |
| `NVDA` | 5.2 | 0.114 | 3.12% | −5.85 | 0.028 | 0.518 | 0.997 |

**Takeaways:** **γ > 0 every ticker** confirms M2's leverage effect inside the model; **ν ≈ 5** confirms fat
tails. The **standardised-residual Ljung-Box p-values (all > 0.29) and squared (all > 0.77)** show the AR+GJR-
GARCH **jointly whitened the mean and removed the ARCH** — a clean fit (and the proper rebuttal to the raw-resid
LB≈0 in §3). The low MZ R² (forecast variance explains little of *squared-return* realized variance) reflects
how noisy a 1-day realized-variance proxy is, not a bad model. GARCH's mean-forecast row in §6 (DirAcc 0.555) is
shown for completeness; its real value is the variance/interval forecast, not point return.

## 5. Global attention-LSTM — now trained (20 epochs)

flag auto-enables the real 20-epoch run on a Colab GPU (2-epoch smoke on local CPU); see
[`reports/COLAB_TRAINING_GUIDE.md`](../COLAB_TRAINING_GUIDE.md).

**Real result (20-epoch, CPU-verified locally; Colab reproduces):**

| LSTM+Attention | RMSE | DirAcc | DirAcc p |
|---|---|---|---|
| **val** | 0.02083 | **0.539** | **0.036 (sig)** |
| **holdout** | 0.02086 | **0.542** | **0.005 (sig)** — but RMSE worse than naive (DM p=0.002) |

### Backtest (illustrative — NOT investment advice)

^GSPC, long next day when ARIMA > 0 else cash, **212 long-days, 175 position switches**:

| | terminal | ann ret | Sharpe | maxDD |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| buy & hold | ×1.267 | 17.3% | **0.99** | −18.9% |
| strategy 0 bps | ×1.274 | 17.7% | 1.18 | −10.3% |
| strategy 5 bps | ×1.167 | 11.3% | 0.75 | −10.5% |
| strategy 10 bps | ×1.070 | 4.9% | 0.33 | −12.7% |

At **zero cost** the strategy edges buy-and-hold on Sharpe (1.18 vs 0.99) — but it switches position **175
times**, so at a realistic **10 bps/switch it collapses to ×1.070 (Sharpe 0.33)**, far below passive. And the
strategy's daily returns are **not significantly positive** (t=1.38, p=0.168). Conclusion: no tradeable edge
once costs and significance are accounted for.
