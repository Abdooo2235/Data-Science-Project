# Milestone 6 — Volatility Regime Classification (calm vs stormy)

**Project:** Stock Market Trend Analysis | **Notebook:** `notebooks/05_volatility_regime.py` | **Seed:** 42
**Reads:** `train_fe` + `val_fe` for the sweep; `holdout_fe` opened **exactly once** at §6.
**Not investment advice.** A calm/stormy call is a *risk* estimate (position sizing, option pricing, VaR),
**not** a direction-of-price signal and **not** a profit signal. **">75% here does not mean >75% at
predicting whether you make money."**

## Why this milestone exists (the honest answer to "make it >75%")

The recurring ask was: *make the model predict up/down at >75%.* On these four liquid tickers that is not
honestly reachable — M3/M4 proved next-day **return direction** is an efficient-market coin flip (~50–54%,
all upward drift), and Kaggle notebooks showing 90% are leaking (predicting price *level*, or scaling on the
full series). So M6 answers a **different, honest question** — the original (>75% on price direction) stays
unreachable:

> Not "will the price go up tomorrow?" (unpredictable) but **"will the next h days be calm or stormy?"**

Volatility **clusters** (M2: ACF of squared returns lag-1 = +0.487; leverage effect; ARCH-LM significant on
all tickers), and M3.6 already proved realized vol is predictable out-of-sample as a *regression* (QLIKE, R²
up to 0.59). **M6 is the accuracy-framed communication of that M3.6 result** — it repackages the same
predictable volatility as a classification accuracy so the ">75%" ask has a concrete number. It is not an
independent finding, and it is deliberately less rigorous than M3.6 (M3.6's QLIKE / Diebold–Mariano / HAR-RV
machinery is the authoritative version).

## Target (reuses M1/M3.6's leakage-safe `fwd_rv_h`)

`fwd_rv_h` = sqrt( Σ log_return² over days t+1 … t+h ), per ticker, per split (last h rows NaN). Each day is
labelled by its forward vol relative to **train-only** per-ticker cutoffs:

| cut scheme | STORMY (1) | CALM (0) | dropped (ambiguous) |
|---|---|---|---|
| `median` | top 50% | bottom 50% | none (100% coverage, hardest split) |
| `tercile` | top third | bottom third | middle third |
| `quintile` | top 20% | bottom 20% | middle 60% (clearest split) |

All three are reported so the headline is **not** cherry-picked. Horizons **h = 5** and **h = 20**.

## Models (and the baseline that carries the result)

- **majority-class baseline** — predict the more common regime every day (the floor).
- **vol-persistence baseline (the key one)** — predict STORMY iff the **trailing** h-day realized vol
  `trail_rv_h = sqrt(Σ of the past h squared returns)` is above the train cutoff. This is exactly M3.6's
  RandomWalk-vol and is **scale-matched to the target** (same construction, backward window). Encodes "vol is
  sticky" with zero learning. *(An earlier draft used the per-day `realized_vol_21` here — a units bug
  (~σ vs ~√h·σ) that made persistence never fire at h=20 and collapse to the majority class, faking a huge
  model edge. Caught by the Investment Researcher audit and fixed; see Corrections.)*
- **LightGBM classifier** — on the 39 `MODEL_FEATURES` + ticker (categorical), deterministic, seed 42.

## Results — holdout (sealed 2025→2026, opened once)

Fit on train+val, train-only cutoffs, scored on the clear (non-ambiguous) holdout rows.

| h | cut | clear n | coverage | base rate | majority | **persistence** | LightGBM | ROC-AUC |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | median | 1364 | 100% | 0.53 | 0.528 | 0.592 | 0.585 | 0.684 |
| 5 | tercile | 797 | 58% | 0.51 | 0.507 | 0.631 | 0.650 | 0.753 |
| 5 | quintile | 409 | 30% | 0.49 | 0.506 | 0.714 | **0.741** | 0.862 |
| 20 | median | 1304 | 100% | 0.54 | 0.535 | 0.584 | 0.580 | 0.659 |
| 20 | tercile | 708 | 54% | 0.53 | 0.534 | 0.669 | 0.705 | 0.806 |
| 20 | quintile | 354 | 27% | 0.48 | 0.517 | **0.836** | 0.782 | 0.904 |

**Headline (honest): volatility regime is 71%–84% predictable out-of-sample at the calm/stormy extremes —
and the predictor is persistence, not the model.** At h=20 quintile a trivial sticky-vol rule scores
**0.836**; LightGBM scores 0.782 and **does not beat it**. LightGBM only edges persistence by a hair where it
does at all (h=5 quintile 0.741 vs 0.714; h=20 tercile 0.705 vs 0.669). So the ">75%" the user wanted is real
and reachable — on *risk regime*, via a well-understood mechanism — but it is **vol clustering, not machine
learning**, and emphatically not price direction. This is fully consistent with M3.6, where RandomWalk-vol was
already a strong floor that models beat only modestly on QLIKE.

Per ticker (h=20 quintile, LightGBM): AMZN 0.875 (n=56), NVDA 0.848 (n=151), ^GSPC 0.847 (n=59), AAPL 0.568
(n=88). Positive on 3 of 4 names, but the per-ticker n are small (2 of the 4 under 60), so "broad across the
cross-section" is suggestive, not established; AAPL is essentially chance.

## Honest reading (the caveats, stated up front)

1. **The signal is real; the model is not the source of it.** Both persistence and LightGBM sit far above the
   ~0.50 base rate, so regime *is* predictable. But persistence alone gets there — LightGBM adds little and at
   the headline cell (h=20 quintile) is slightly *worse*. The value is in the phenomenon (vol clustering), not
   in a clever classifier.
2. **The h=20 headline is not validation-corroborated; h=5 is.** The h=20 point estimate is the highest, but
   the cell was chosen by argmax on the holdout, and on val h=20 was a coin flip: 2024 was a compressed-vol
   year, so only **10% CALM / 11% STORMY** of its forward-20d vol reached the train quintile extremes (vs 20/20
   on train, 14/13 on holdout) — a tiny, low-power val sample, not evidence against the effect. The
   **val-corroborated** result is **h=5 quintile** (monotone on both val 0.61→0.62→0.65 and holdout 0.714 /
   0.741). Read h=20 as suggestive, h=5 as established.
3. **Coverage/overlap disclosed.** Sharper cut ⇒ higher accuracy, lower coverage (median ~0.58 @100% →
   quintile ~0.78–0.84 @~27%); excluded middle-vol days are genuinely ambiguous and reported as excluded.
   Overlapping h-day windows are handled two ways: a **moving-block bootstrap** (block=h) puts the LightGBM
   h=20 headline at 95% CI **[0.64, 0.90]** (lower bound still clears the base rate, but the interval spans
   the 0.75 line — so "clears 0.75" is a point estimate, not a significance claim), and a **thinned** re-score
   (every h-th clear row) keeps the effect but on small n (h=20 n≈19), so it corroborates direction only.
4. **What this is NOT.** A calm/stormy flag is a *risk* forecast, not tradeable return alpha and not a profit
   signal. And at 27% coverage / a 1-month binary it is a weak *risk instrument* too: option pricing wants a
   continuous vol surface, position sizing and VaR want a distribution — all better served by **M3.6's
   continuous vol forecast**, which is the actually-useful deliverable. M6's classifier is a pedagogical
   accuracy framing of that same predictability, not a new trading or risk tool. Consistent with the M3/M4 EMH
   conclusion on returns.

## Methods & leakage discipline (identical bar to M3.6)

- Cutoffs fit on **train only** (`cutoffs()` only ever receives `train`), applied unchanged to val/holdout.
- The val sweep fits on **train only**; the holdout fits on **train+val**. Fitting on the eval split was a real
  bug caught pre-holdout (giveaway ~1.0 val accuracy) and fixed by threading `fit_df`.
- Persistence baseline uses `trail_rv_h`, scale-matched to the target (fixed from a per-day-vol units bug).
- All features are past-only `MODEL_FEATURES` (39). Holdout read on exactly one gated line (`OPEN_HOLDOUT`).
- Metrics unit-tested; per-ticker + thinned re-score + block-bootstrap CI reported; scores persisted to
  `models/m6_{val,holdout}_scores.csv`, `m6_holdout_per_ticker.csv`, `m6_coverage_curve_val.csv`.

## Corrections (from the multi-agent audit)

- **HIGH (Investment Researcher) — persistence baseline mis-scaled → degenerate.** Original persistence used
  per-day `realized_vol_21` (~σ) against a summed `fwd_rv_20` cutoff (~√20·σ), so it never predicted STORMY at
  h=20 and collapsed to the majority class — making LightGBM look ~26 points better than persistence when it
  is actually **worse**. Fixed with a scale-matched `trail_rv_h` (M3.6's RandomWalk-vol). **This flipped the
  milestone's conclusion** from "LightGBM classifies regimes with skill" to "regime is predictable via
  persistence; the model adds little" — the honest result.
- **MED-HIGH — headline was argmax-on-holdout at a val-failing cell.** Now the h=20 number is explicitly
  labelled "highest point estimate, not val-corroborated," and **h=5 quintile is named as the established,
  val-corroborated result.**
- **MED — no CI / significance.** Added a moving-block bootstrap CI (block=h); reworded the self-audit so
  "≥0.75" is a point estimate, not a significance claim (effective n at h=20 is ~19).
- **MED — redundant with M3.6, less rigorous.** M6 is now framed explicitly as the accuracy-communication of
  M3.6, which remains the authoritative (QLIKE/DM/HAR) version.
- **MED — overstated economic use.** Use-case claims softened; M3.6's continuous forecast named as the real
  risk deliverable.
- **LOW — "non-overlapping" overstated → renamed "thinned re-score"; breadth claim softened (small per-ticker
  n, AAPL ≈ chance); "answered a different question" caveat added to the disclaimer.**
- *(Model QA audit independently confirmed the same scale-mismatch before its run hit an API error.)*
