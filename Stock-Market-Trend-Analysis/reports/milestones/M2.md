`seasonal_decompose` on ^GSPC, run on the **trading-day ordinal index** (trading days are irregular in calendar
time, so the x-axis is the trading-day sequence): additive `period=5` (week), multiplicative `period=252` (year).

**Takeaway:** the **trend dominates**. Weekly seasonal amplitude is **2.97 pts ≈ 0.11%** of the price range;

seasonality** → a SARIMA seasonal term on prices would add little; the exploitable structure is in the
*variance of returns* (Section 5), not a calendar season.

## 5. Autocorrelation — returns vs squared returns — Figs 11–13

- **Fig 11** `M2_fig11_acf_returns.png` (ACF of returns) · **Fig 12** `M2_fig12_pacf_returns.png` (PACF) ·
  **Fig 13** `M2_fig13_acf_sq_returns.png` (ACF of **squared** returns).

| ^GSPC | lag 1 | lag 2 | lag 5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| ACF(return) | **−0.162** | +0.104 | +0.064 |
| ACF(return²) | **+0.487** | +0.535 | +0.320 |

**Takeaway (corrected):** the lag-1 return ACF is **not** ~0 — it is **−0.162** for ^GSPC (≈4σ past the
±0.045 band; also significant negative for AAPL −0.087, NVDA −0.078). The **negative sign** is a
mean-reversion / **bid-ask-bounce microstructure** signature, not return alpha (for ^GSPC, an index with no
bid-ask, it points to stale-component effects). It justifies trying a low-order **ARIMA(1,0,0)/(0,0,1)** but
is unlikely to beat a naive baseline net of costs. The **squared-return ACF is large and slow-decaying**
(lag-1 = 0.487) → **volatility clustering confirmed**. Mean near-unpredictable; variance highly predictable —
the single most important result for model selection.

## 6. Stationarity (ADF) + heteroskedasticity (ARCH-LM, Ljung-Box r²)

| Ticker | ADF Close p | ADF ΔClose p | ADF log_return p | ARCH-LM p | LB(r², 10) p |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `^GSPC` | 0.8278 | 0.0000 | 0.0000 | **0.0000** | **0.0000** |
| `AAPL` | 0.9399 | 0.0000 | 0.0000 | **0.0000** | **0.0000** |
| `AMZN` | 0.5329 | 0.0000 | 0.0000 | **0.0000** | **0.0000** |
| `NVDA` | 0.9978 | 0.0000 | 0.0000 | **0.0000** | **0.0000** |

**Takeaway:** prices non-stationary; **returns stationary in mean** (ARIMA `d=0`). But ARCH-LM and Ljung-Box(r²)
reject constant variance for **every** ticker → returns are **conditionally heteroskedastic**. And the variance
response is **asymmetric** (leverage effect, H5/Fig 18) → the M3 variance model should be **EGARCH/GJR-GARCH**,
not symmetric GARCH(1,1).

| Feature | Mutual info | Pearson |

The top vol feature's MI is **~67× the noise floor and ~3× the best linear feature** — invisible in a Pearson

crises → diversification fails when most needed. Next-day *mean* is weakly predictable (EMH), but next-day
*volatility* is strongly predictable (MI + clustering).

## 8. Hypotheses (state → test → verdict → implication)

| # | Hypothesis | Test | Result | Verdict | Implication for M3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| H1 | Returns are normal | Jarque-Bera per ticker | JB 2,129–21,958, p≈0 | **REJECTED** (all) | Gaussian-error ARIMA misspecified → consider Student-t errors |
| H2 | Volatility clusters | ARCH-LM (lag 10) | LM 65–757, p≈0 | **CONFIRMED** (all) | Variance model justified |
| H3 | Tech corr with ^GSPC > 0.6 | Pearson r | AAPL 0.78, AMZN 0.65, NVDA 0.66 | **YES** (all) | Global model can share cross-series signal |
| H4 | January effect | Welch t-test **per ticker** | AMZN t=2.24 p=0.027; ^GSPC/AAPL/NVDA p>0.4 | **AMZN only** | No robust market-wide effect; keep `month` for LSTM regardless |
| H5 | Leverage (asymmetric vol) | \|next-day return\| after down vs up | down>up all 4, p 7e-6…0.035 | **ASYMMETRIC** (all) | **EGARCH/GJR-GARCH**, not symmetric GARCH(1,1) |

> **H4 note:** the earlier *pooled* test (t=1.63, p=0.103) was statistically invalid — 4 tickers correlated at
> ~0.65 are not independent observations (~3× variance inflation), and pooling masked AMZN's individual
> significance. Corrected to per-ticker above.

## 9. Modelling implications (hand-off to M3)

- **Target & transform:** forecast `target_log_return` (next-day). ARIMA on the **return** series, `d=0`
  (returns already mean-stationary).
- **ARIMA order:** return ACF/PACF near zero → **small p, q** (try (1,0,1), (2,0,2), AR/MA around lag 1).
  High orders will overfit noise. The −0.162 lag-1 ACF is the only non-trivial mean structure.
- **Variance is the real signal → asymmetric GARCH:** ARCH-LM + ACF(r²) + the leverage effect (H5) demand an
  **EGARCH/GJR-GARCH** alongside ARIMA. ARIMA-on-mean alone barely beats the naive baseline.
- **LSTM justification = MI, not Pearson:** vol features carry ~67× the MI noise floor while being invisible to
  Pearson — that nonlinear, vol-based signal is what the **global LSTM** (ticker one-hot + 30 `MODEL_FEATURES`)
  can exploit and ARIMA cannot.
- **Per-ticker vs global:** **per-ticker ARIMA + asymmetric GARCH (GJR implemented; EGARCH an alternative)** (4 sets) + **one global LSTM** exploiting cross-series
  co-movement (H3).
- **Suggested extra features for M3:** a **downside/semi-deviation vol** (leverage effect) and a
  **volatility-regime flag** (clear calm-vs-turbulent regimes in Fig 7) — neither is in the current 30 features.
- **Metric:** RMSE/MAE on returns **and directional accuracy** — realistic target 52–55% hit rate. Naive
  baseline first.
- **Feature hygiene:** train on `MODEL_FEATURES` only; `dropna(subset=MODEL_FEATURES)`; never touch
  val/holdout until M3's final holdout step.
